Freshwater Aquariums

Neon Tetra Fish

The Neon Tetra is a popular and vibrant species known for its striking blue and red coloration, making it a favorite among aquarists. This small, peaceful freshwater fish is native to the Amazon River Basin in South America and is well-suited for community tanks. The brilliant blue iridescent stripe along the body, paired with the glowing red tail, gives the Neon Tetra its signature "neon" glow.

Meet Our Aquatic Animals

Our Amazing Animals

At Nexus Aquarium, we believe every animal has a story to tell. Whether it's the playful antics of dolphins, the colorful beauty of tropical fish, or the incredible intelligence of sea turtles, our animal exhibits offer a closer look at the wonders of the natural world. Below, explore some of the fascinating species that call Nexus Aquarium home.

Marine Mammals

Dolphins

Known for their playful nature and high intelligence, dolphins are a favorite among visitors. These marine mammals are skilled hunters, using echolocation to communicate and locate food. Our Dolphin Discovery exhibit allows you to witness these beautiful creatures interact with their trainers in real-time.

Sea Lions

Sea lions are incredibly social animals that thrive in groups. Visitors can watch them perform in our interactive shows and learn about their playful behavior. They also help to maintain a balanced ecosystem in the wild by controlling fish populations.

Tropical Fishs

Clownfish

Famous for their symbiotic relationship with sea anemones, clownfish are colorful and curious creatures that thrive in tropical coral reefs. Watch them interact with their host anemones, providing a perfect example of mutualism in nature.

Neon Tetras

These small, brightly colored freshwater fish are a favorite among aquarium enthusiasts. Their vibrant blue and red bodies make them a stunning addition to any aquatic setup. They are peaceful creatures that do well in schools, often found in the wild in slow-moving rivers and streams in South America.

Reptiles

Sea Turtles

The sea turtles in our care are part of an important conservation program. These gentle creatures can live for decades and are often found navigating the oceanâ€™s vast expanses. Visitors can learn about the challenges they face in the wild, including habitat destruction and the effects of climate change.

Alligators

Alligators are impressive apex predators found in both freshwater and brackish waters. Our Alligator Bay exhibit allows you to observe these powerful creatures up close. They play a crucial role in maintaining healthy ecosystems by controlling populations of fish, birds, and other wildlife.

Invertebrates

Octopus

The octopus is one of the most intelligent invertebrates in the world. Known for their ability to change color and shape to blend into their surroundings, octopuses are masters of camouflage. Our interactive exhibits let visitors explore the unique behaviors of these fascinating creatures.

Jellyfish

Jellyfish may seem delicate, but they are highly efficient predators in the ocean. With their translucent bodies and mesmerizing movements, theyâ€™re an aquatic wonder. Our exhibit features different species of jellyfish, giving visitors a chance to admire their beauty while learning about their vital role in marine ecosystems.

Conservation Efforts

At Nexus Aquarium, we are dedicated to the conservation of marine life and the protection of endangered species. Through our partnerships with global marine protection organizations, we work to educate the public about sustainable practices and the importance of preserving our oceans and freshwater habitats. Our animal care team is actively involved in rehabilitation programs for injured sea turtles and other species in need of care.
